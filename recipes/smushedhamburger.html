Smushed Hamburgers

A southern classic born from hard times

This hearty dish hails from my father's mother and her family. At first glance many may assume we're just delving into the classic of a sloppy joe, but fear not, this recipe is far simpler and allows good ingredients to shine!

With ground beef, onions and a few simple spices, this recipe is sure to please.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of 90/20 Ground Beef
  • 1 teaspoon Kosher Salt, with more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, with more to taste
  • One small yellow onion, finely diced
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • A quarter cup of water
  • and my own contribution to this meaty mess, one small pinch of MSG

Steps

  1. Begin by bringing a medium sized skillet or cast iron pan to medium heat
  2. Add ground beef and diced onions and cook until meat is brown and the onions have gone clear
  3. Add all of the above spices
  4. Add water and lit simmer
  5. Once the water has cooked off, taste your meat and adjust salt/pepper according to your personal likings
  6. Enjoy!

Tips!

Adding a little water after the meat is browned and the onions are translucent gives the flavors time to meld as the water cooks off, this step isn't required but suggested.
